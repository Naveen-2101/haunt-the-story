# H A U N T # T H E S T O R Y ## CHAPTER: 1 ### PART: 3 — “THE SECRET” The moment the girl disappeared into the ground, an unnatural silence spread across the park. No wind blew. No leaves rustled. It felt as if the entire place had stopped breathing at once. Just a few seconds earlier, the park had been ruled by screams, darkness, and freezing terror. But now, it looked completely normal again. The streetlights had become stable, the swings hung motionless, and the benches stood perfectly in place. But the normality only existed on the surface. It didn’t feel normal at all. All of us were still standing there. Nobody moved forward. Nobody stepped back. It felt as though our feet had been nailed to the ground. We wanted to run, but our bodies refused to respond. The only thing we could hear was the sound of our own heartbeats. It felt like if even one of us breathed too loudly, the silence would shatter… and something would return. Ansh slowly placed his hand over his ear and whispered, “Bro… do you also feel like someone is still watching us?” Harsh answered without even looking at him, “Not feel… I’m sure.” Ayush nervously laughed and said, “So… the ghost left, right? Or was this just her final prank?” Nobody answered him. His laugh died in the air. I looked down at the ground. The place where the girl had stood still looked disturbed, as if something underneath had moved the soil from below. Cold air continued rising from that spot. The strangest thing was that the dirt still hadn’t settled properly. It almost looked like something underneath it was still breathing. Without telling anyone, I quietly stepped back. Harsh slowly said, “I don’t think standing here any longer is a good idea.” Ansh immediately added, “Yeah… it feels like something bad will happen again.” But even then, none of us moved. It felt like we had been trapped inside some invisible boundary. That was when my eyes fell on Kalanga. He stood a little far away from us. Completely calm. There was no fear in his body language, no surprise in his eyes. It felt like this wasn’t the first time he had seen something like this. But the strange part was that he wasn’t looking at the park. He was looking at us. Almost as if he was checking whether we were still the same people… or if something inside us had already changed. Ansh stepped forward slightly and asked, “So… what now?” Without raising his voice, Kalanga replied, “Now… you will begin to understand.” His voice sounded normal, but those words sent a cold shiver down my spine. *Begin to understand?* Did that mean everything we had seen so far was only the beginning? Ayush nervously took out his phone. The screen was working perfectly. The time was normal. Full network. WhatsApp notifications kept appearing — memes, group messages, random chats. He looked confused and muttered, “Bro… if everything is normal… then how did all this even happen?” Nobody answered. In the distance, two people walked past the park laughing and talking casually, like it was a completely ordinary night. They passed right beside us, yet they didn’t notice anything strange. Not the broken atmosphere. Not us. It was as if, for those few moments, we didn’t even exist in their world. And then I understood. Everything that had happened… had happened only to us. For the rest of the world, this was just another normal park. Harsh quietly said, “DD and Sai…” The moment he spoke their names, the air suddenly felt colder again. Ansh muttered, “They’re not here… and I don’t think that’s a good sign.” A heavy feeling settled inside my chest. Without them, the park felt emptier than before, as if something more than people had been taken away. For the first time, Kalanga lowered his head slightly. As though some old memory had suddenly become heavy on his shoulders. He said nothing. But his silence itself felt like a warning. Like this peace wasn’t going to last very long. The park looked normal again. But we weren’t normal anymore. And this wasn’t just fear from one terrible night. This was the beginning of a story that had now started following us. In a low voice, I asked Kalanga, “What is all this? And why didn’t those people notice us?” Kalanga turned toward us and calmly replied, “The girl does everything inside another world.” Ansh frowned immediately. “What are you even talking about? I don’t understand.” Kalanga looked at him coldly and said, “I won’t repeat myself. So listen carefully.” He paused. “Everything that happened with us tonight… did not happen in this world. It happened in another one.” All of us stared at him in confusion. “And that world,” he continued, “looks exactly like this one.” Hearing that only made us more confused. Then Kalanga turned around and started walking away. “Follow me,” he said. The moment we left the park, Kalanga continued walking silently through the darkness. He never looked back. He never explained where we were going. We all exchanged nervous looks, silently asking each other the same question: *Where are we even going?* But none of us had the courage to stay behind. The night grew darker with every step. Streetlights became fewer. The noise of the city slowly disappeared until only our footsteps and heartbeats remained. After walking for some time, we reached a place where the city itself seemed to end. In front of us stood an old warehouse. Broken walls. Rusted iron gates. Loose wires hanging from the roof. It looked like time itself had stopped there years ago. Outside the warehouse hung an old broken signboard. The name was almost impossible to read. It felt like nobody had stepped into that place for decades. Ayush slowly muttered, “Bro… this place probably looks haunted even on Google Maps.” Kalanga stopped. Without even touching the gate— **KRRRREEEKKK—** The iron gate slowly opened by itself. The moment we stepped inside, a cold rotten gust of air hit our faces. The darkness was so deep our eyes needed time to adjust. Inside the warehouse were old crates, iron drums, and strange chalk symbols drawn all over the floor. Circles inside triangles. Symbols almost identical to the ones we had seen in the park. Ansh looked around nervously. “This place… doesn’t feel normal.” Kalanga answered, “Because this place was never meant for normal people.” At the center of the warehouse was a large open space. Moonlight fell through a hole in the roof, illuminating a massive black circle drawn on the ground. It looked exactly like the one from the park. But this one felt deeper. Stronger. More dangerous. I finally asked, “Kalanga… what is this place?” He slowly replied, “This warehouse is not just a warehouse.” He looked directly at us. “This is a training ground.” Ayush almost shouted, “Training ground?! Bro, this looks more like a final boss arena!” Kalanga ignored him completely. “Your lives will change here,” he said calmly. “Here, you will understand what fear truly is… and how to control it.” We all immediately started asking questions at once. “What training?” “Who are you?” “What is happening to us?” For the first time, Kalanga smiled slightly. “Fine,” he said. “Now I’ll tell you everything.” He looked directly at us. “You only know the girl’s story.” Then he paused. “You don’t know mine.” Ansh immediately replied, “We don’t even know the girl’s story properly!” Kalanga gave a faint smile. “Patience. You’ll know everything.” Then his expression became serious again. “Years ago,” Kalanga began, “before the school was abandoned… I worked there as the night watchman.” Harsh almost interrupted him in shock. “Wait… you were always there?” Kalanga nodded slowly. “I knew Anaya since she was a child. She used to call me ‘Kalanga Chacha.’ Whenever her grandparents went to the hospital, she stayed near the school after classes.” Something inside my chest tightened painfully. “That night…” Kalanga’s voice became heavier, “…the night everything changed… I was on duty.” He stared at the floor while speaking. “Around nine at night, I heard sounds coming from behind the school. I ran there…” His fingers trembled slightly. “But I was too late.” “Anaya was lying on the ground.” He paused. “And standing near her… was a boy named Aryan.” All of us froze. “There was blood all over his hands.” Then Kalanga immediately looked toward me. “The name matching yours is only a coincidence,” he said quickly. “But the sin was real.” “That boy escaped on his bike before I could stop him. I barely even knew him.” Kalanga’s voice almost broke. “And Anaya died in my arms.” Harsh slowly asked, “But why did he kill her?” Kalanga shook his head. “Only Anaya knows that answer now. Maybe even she doesn’t anymore.” Gathering courage, I asked, “When we first went to the school… why didn’t she attack me? Why did she possess Sai instead?” Kalanga answered immediately. “Because your fear wasn’t complete yet.” Then he looked toward the darkness. “But Sai’s mind was already broken. He was weak. That’s why she chose him.” Ayush nervously laughed and said, “So mental strength matters too?” Without smiling, Kalanga replied, “Everything matters here, kid.” Ayush grinned weakly. “Wow. New nickname unlocked.” Kalanga ignored him and continued. “That night, after Anaya died, I prayed to God for help.” He lowered his eyes. “But nobody answered.” Then his voice became colder. “So I asked the darkness for help instead.” Ayush whispered nervously, “Bro… this sounds like a horror movie deal.” Kalanga smiled faintly. “Worse.” He pointed somewhere beneath the warehouse. “There was an ancient temple beneath the school. A place where forbidden rituals and dark practices were once performed.” His voice became heavier. “I sacrificed my life, my name, and my future.” “And after that…” He slowly looked up. “I became what I am today.” Ansh shook his head in disbelief. “Bro… this is getting way too interesting. Someone could literally make a movie on this.” For the first time, Kalanga laughed softly. “Maybe after all this is over.” Harsh directly asked, “Then why don’t you destroy Anaya yourself?” Kalanga closed his eyes for a moment. “Because the night I accepted these powers… I swore I would never destroy an innocent soul.” Then he looked toward us again. “But Anaya has become too powerful now. She can no longer tell the difference between the guilty… and the people caught in between.” I quietly asked, “Then why us?” Kalanga answered without hesitation. “Because all of you have entered her circle.” “You have seen her.” “You know her story.” Then his eyes locked onto mine. “And one of you shares the same name as her murderer.” My heart pounded heavily inside my chest. “That is why she is hunting you the most.” Harsh finally asked the question all of us were thinking. “But how are we supposed to defeat her? She’s too powerful.” Kalanga raised his hand slightly. “We’ll talk about that later.” Then his expression changed. “First, let me tell you about the most important person in this story.” “Dev.” For the first time, there was tension even in Kalanga’s voice. “Dev is a mystery to all of you,” he said. “But understand one thing clearly.” “He is not the villain.” Ayush looked shocked. “So… he’s gonna become our friend?” Kalanga gave a small smile. “Friend? Maybe.” “Or maybe he’ll become the biggest weapon you have.” Then he explained everything slowly. * Dev was once a student of Anaya’s grandfather. * He studied ancient manuscripts and forbidden writings connected to the school. * He possesses knowledge and abilities capable of temporarily containing Anaya’s powers. * But Dev has no personal hatred toward Anaya. His job is only to maintain balance. “He was never involved in Anaya’s murder,” Kalanga emphasized. “But if you truly want to stop her… you will need Dev.” Then he added, “Dev possesses shadow powers capable of binding spirits temporarily. Without him, your chances of survival are almost zero.” I stared at him in disbelief. “Shadow powers? So… we fight physically while Dev supports us with magic?” Kalanga nodded. “Exactly.” “You fight. Dev supports. He will be the center of strategy.” Harsh finally sounded slightly hopeful. “So somebody’s actually going to guide us?” Kalanga’s tone became sharp again. “You cannot be guided until you face your own fear.” “Dev will identify your fears and neutralize them… so that one day you can fight Anaya directly.” Outside the warehouse, the night had grown even darker. The road was completely empty except for one flickering streetlight. The smell of rust, dirt, and forgotten lives filled the air. All four of us stood silently while Kalanga remained calm… almost as if he already knew someone was coming. Then suddenly— **VROOOOOOMMMMM—** The roar of a heavy bike tore through the darkness. The sound was so loud that the warehouse windows shook violently. The bike skidded to a stop near us. Sparks flew from the tires. Dust rose into the air. The first thing we noticed was the glowing red tip of a cigar. Smoke slowly rose upward… and from within that smoke stepped out a man. Dev. Heavy boots. Worn leather jacket. Messy hair. Eyes so tired it looked like life itself had exhausted him. A cigar rested casually between his lips. He switched off the bike. **CLICK.** Then casually threw the chain onto the ground. **CHRRRAAAK.** Dev looked around slowly at the warehouse, the broken walls, the darkness. “Hm.” He took a deep drag from the cigar. “Nice place.” Then he looked at Kalanga and said, “Bro… why do you always choose places like this? Are you allergic to normal cafés or something?” Kalanga stayed silent. Then Dev casually muttered, “So… which idiot disturbed the balance this time?” His tone sounded relaxed, almost joking… but hidden beneath it was a warning sharp enough to cut through steel. Then his eyes finally shifted toward us. He looked at us from head to toe without blinking once. Then he smirked slightly. “Ohhh… so these are the idiot kids who ruined my night?” Ayush’s face went pale. Ansh stepped backward slightly. Harsh stood stiffly still. My heart started pounding harder. Dev’s words sounded like a joke… but it felt like he could kill someone in the very next second if he wanted to. He spun the cigar between his fingers and casually said, “Relax. As long as I’m still using insults, the situation is under control.” Then he looked around the warehouse again. “By the way,” Dev said, “who owns this place? Kalanga, you didn’t ruin another person’s life again, did you?” Kalanga calmly replied, “Focus, Dev.” Dev rolled his eyes dramatically. “Yeah, yeah… same old lecture.” Then suddenly he changed the topic completely. “You know what the most dangerous thing in this city is?” Dev asked casually. None of us answered. Dev smirked slightly. “Secrets.” “A gun can kill someone. A knife can make blood flow.” Then his smile faded. “But secrets…” “…they hollow out entire lives.” He dropped the cigar onto the floor and crushed it beneath his boot. Then he looked at Kalanga again. “So you’re still doing the same thing?” he asked quietly. “Pretending you can fix broken people?” Kalanga answered slowly, “Someone has to.” Dev became serious for the first time. “Yeah.” “Otherwise everyone dies.” “And this time…” “…the list is very long.” All of us exchanged nervous glances. Then, for the first time, Dev looked directly into our eyes. “Don’t bother telling me your names,” he said. “I already know who you are.” My heartbeat became louder. “You’re probably wondering,” Dev continued, “who I am, why I act like this, and why all this drama exists.” He adjusted his jacket casually. “Simple.” “When people become too scared to even speak anymore…” “…that’s when they call me.” Then he smirked. “I’m the guy who makes ghosts think twice before showing up.” Finally, Dev turned toward Kalanga again. “So,” he asked quietly, “How far has she gone?” Kalanga took a deep breath. “Anaya is no longer the same.” For one second, something changed in Dev’s eyes. Anger. Or maybe grief. “…Damn.” Dev looked away. “I warned you this would happen.” Ayush finally gathered enough courage to ask, “Who… who exactly are you?” Dev burst out laughing. “Didn’t I just explain that?” Then he stepped closer. “I’m the last option, understood, kid?” Ayush nervously laughed. “Another nickname…” Dev smirked. “Stay around me longer and I’ll give you so many nicknames that you’ll forget your real one.” Then suddenly, his expression became serious again. In a cold voice, he said: “I was not responsible for Anaya’s murder.” “But whatever happened after that…” “…maybe I’m not completely innocent either.” The air inside the warehouse suddenly felt heavier.
