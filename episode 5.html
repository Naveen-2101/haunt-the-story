# **HAUNT THE STORY** ## **CHAPTER: 1 PART: 5** On the deserted road, when the shadow standing under the streetlight finally moved completely… the air itself seemed to scream. For the first time, Dev’s smile disappeared. His eyes turned serious—exactly like a hunter staring at his prey. The shadow slowly began taking shape, and within a moment, Anaya was standing there completely. Her face was half normal, half burned—as if memories and death were fighting inside the same body. Her feet were not touching the ground, her hair floated upward unnaturally, and in her eyes there was only one thing— Hatred. Hatred so deep that even the leaves on the road began trembling. “You…” Anaya’s voice echoed in multiple voices at once, “you know it, don’t you… people like you are the ones who destroy balance.” Dev let out a cold laugh, adjusted the collar of his jacket, and replied, “Balance? BC, you’ve turned the whole world upside down and you’re talking about balance?” In one sudden movement, Anaya appeared right in front of Dev. So close that her freezing breath touched his ear. “You are not afraid?” she whispered. Dev looked directly into her eyes. “I am… but I got used to it.” ## **BOOM!** The very next second, Anaya raised her hand and the entire road seemed to split apart. Black smoke-like creatures burst out from the ground and lunged toward Dev like snakes. Streetlights exploded one after another— *fssshh… DHADAAAM!* —and the entire road drowned in darkness. But inside that darkness, one thing glowed. A black symbol ignited on Dev’s hand—as if the shadows themselves obeyed him. “Mistake,” Dev said slowly, “you thought I was a normal human.” He slammed his foot against the ground. # **SHADOW RIFT OPEN!** The ground behind Dev cracked open, forming a deep black pit from which shadow chains burst out toward Anaya. Anaya screamed—but it was not pain. It was rage. She spread her arms and shattered the chains like threads. “I AM MEMORY!” Anaya roared. “I AM DEATH!” Then she used her most dangerous attack. The entire area around them began changing. The road disappeared. Suddenly Dev was standing inside the old school corridor. Blood was not dripping from the walls. Memories were. Children laughing. Crying. Breaking sounds. All together. Dev gritted his teeth. “Ahh… memory world. Typical ghost move.” Anaya stood at the end of the corridor, now completely distorted. Sometimes her face looked normal. Sometimes fully burned. “This is where you break,” she said. “Just like everyone else.” Dev closed his eyes… and opened them again. And then the real Dev appeared. Shadows began circling around his body—controlled, disciplined, like soldiers. For the first time, his voice became heavier. “Listen, Anaya… I understand your pain.” A pause. “But that doesn’t mean you get to consume everyone.” He touched an old scar on his wrist. # **BLACK ASCENSION ACTIVATED** Dev’s shadow separated from behind him and stood beside him. It looked exactly like him— except its eyes were completely black. The corridor floor cracked. Anaya tried stepping back… too late. Dev and his shadow raised their hands together. # **SHADOW NULL PULSE** A silent blast exploded. No sound. But the impact shook the entire memory world. Walls shattered. Memories screamed and dissolved into the air. Anaya was thrown backward violently, as if something was being ripped out from inside her. “NO!” Anaya screamed. “I WILL COME BACK!” Dev replied tiredly, “I know. That’s what makes it fun.” Anaya’s body slowly began turning into smoke. For the first time, her eyes held not only anger… but confusion. Then— # **SHE VANISHED** The corridor collapsed. Dev was back on the same deserted road. Streetlights flickered back on. The bike was still dead. The air had become normal again— but the silence was still heavy. Dev took a deep breath and wiped the sweat from his forehead. “BC…” he muttered, “that was just the warning round.” Far away… very far away… a faint laugh echoed. Anaya’s. Dev looked toward the sky and said, “Game on, ghost girl.” And at that moment, he knew— This fight was not over. It had only begun. --- # **WAREHOUSE** Inside the warehouse, heavy silence filled the air. Moonlight entered through the broken parts of the roof. The black circle on the ground was half in light and half in darkness—as if it was hiding its own secret. Aryan, Ansh, Ayush, and Harsh stood near the circle. No one spoke. Kalanga stood before them with both hands behind his back. His face was calm, but there was pressure in his eyes—as if he knew time was running out. For a few seconds, only the sound of the wind could be heard. Then Kalanga slowly said, “The time has come.” Everyone looked at him. Ansh asked, “For what?” Kalanga checked his watch. **11:47 PM** He drew a straight line on the ground using chalk. “Five days later… at this exact time.” Ayush frowned. “Why 11:47?” Kalanga’s voice became heavier. “Because that is the exact time Anaya died.” The silence deepened. Harsh asked quietly, “So she’ll be weaker then?” Kalanga slightly shook his head. “Not weaker… unstable.” “For one minute, she exists neither completely in this world… nor the other.” Aryan asked, “So we attack at that moment?” Kalanga drew five small marks on the ground. “That is why the next five days are for preparation.” Ansh said frustrated, “What preparation? Why not fight directly?” Kalanga stared at him. “Because Anaya is not a normal ghost.” A pause. “If you attack directly… you won’t survive.” Ayush nervously laughed. “Great… so what’s the plan?” Kalanga stepped into the center of the circle. “Starting tomorrow… you will enter Anaya’s memories.” Harsh instantly asked, “Memories? Meaning?” Kalanga explained, “Every powerful ghost is tied to its memories.” “Her life. Her pain. Her death.” “And Anaya’s power comes from them.” Aryan asked slowly, “So we have to enter her memories?” Kalanga nodded. “Separately.” Everyone spoke together. “Separately?!” Kalanga calmly replied, “Each one of you will see one part of her life.” “One memory.” “One truth.” “One reason why Anaya became this.” Ansh asked seriously, “And if we get trapped?” Kalanga answered directly, “Then you won’t return.” Silence. Harsh whispered, “This sounds like a suicide mission.” Kalanga smiled faintly. “When is fighting ghosts ever safe?” Then he looked at all of them one by one. “Rest tonight.” “Tomorrow morning… everything begins.” Aryan asked, “The school?” Kalanga answered immediately, “Yes.” “The same school where Anaya’s story began.” The warehouse lights flickered once. Everyone automatically looked upward. Kalanga slowly said, “From tomorrow… you won’t just see truth.” “You will see Anaya’s pain.” Then he spoke one final line. “And remember one thing.” “What you see… may not only be memory.” “It could also be a trap.” The warehouse fell silent again. And that night, everyone understood one thing— The game was truly about to begin. --- # **DAY 1 – SCHOOL ENTRY** It was 7 AM. Clouds covered the morning light. From a distance alone, the abandoned school looked wrong—as if the building itself was hiding a secret. Aryan, Ansh, Ayush, and Harsh stood outside the gate. The rusty gate made soft sounds with the wind. *krrr… krrr…* Behind them stood Kalanga, completely calm. For a few seconds, nobody spoke. Then Kalanga quietly said, “This is where everything started.” Harsh looked at the building. “To be honest… this place already feels weird.” Ayush tried joking, “If I meet the ghost, I’ll ask for an autograph.” Nobody laughed. Kalanga pushed the gate. # **KREEEEEEAK** The gate slowly opened. As if the school itself was inviting them inside. --- # **ABANDONED CORRIDOR** The corridor inside was long and dark. Broken tiles. Old notice boards. Dust-covered desks. Their footsteps echoed against the walls. Aryan felt pressure—as if someone was watching. Kalanga stopped walking. “Anaya’s strongest memory is inside this building.” Ansh asked, “Which place?” Kalanga pointed forward. # **CLASSROOM 3B** The same class where Anaya studied. The door was half open. Harsh slowly pushed it. # **CREEEAK** Cold air filled the room. Broken desks. Old blackboard. And in one corner, a small desk with a scratched name: # **ANAYA** Aryan stopped near it. He wiped the dust away with his fingers. The air instantly became colder. Kalanga whispered, “This is where the memory gate opens.” Ayush asked confused, “Memory gate?” Kalanga looked at Aryan. “Anaya’s energy is connected to you the most.” Aryan asked, “Because of my name…?” Kalanga nodded. “Exactly.” Then he said, “Touch the desk.” Aryan slowly placed his hand on it. One second. Two seconds. Then suddenly— # **WHOOSH** The entire classroom shook violently. Wind exploded through the room. The walls blurred. “Aryan!” Ansh shouted. But darkness covered Aryan’s vision. --- # **ANAYA’S MEMORY WORLD** When Aryan opened his eyes… he was no longer inside the classroom. He stood in the same school corridor. But this corridor was new. The walls were clean. Children’s voices echoed everywhere. The school was alive. Aryan whispered, “This… is the past.” A girl walked toward him from a distance. Normal school uniform. Long hair. A small smile on her face. Anaya. But not the ghost. The real, living Anaya. She laughed with her friends while walking. Aryan silently watched. Then suddenly— At the other end of the corridor stood a boy. His face hidden in shadow. But his eyes were fixed directly on Anaya. Strange. Very strange. Aryan instantly felt it— This was not normal. Anaya walked toward her classroom, but the boy’s eyes kept following her. Aryan slowly walked toward him. As he got closer, the boy slowly lifted his face. His eyes were cold. And a strange smile spread across his lips. One thought entered Aryan’s mind. This is him. The dark part of Anaya’s story. Suddenly the corridor lights flickered. Children’s voices began distorting. Then Aryan heard a whisper near his ear. Very soft. Very cold. “Why… did you come here…” Aryan’s heart stopped for a second. He slowly turned. At the end of the corridor… inside the shadows… stood Anaya. But this time she was not alive. Her eyes were completely black. And her face slowly transformed into the same burned ghost form. She stared directly at Aryan. Then whispered— “This is my memory.” “No one returns from here.” The corridor lights shut off all at once. And Aryan finally realized— This was not only memory.
