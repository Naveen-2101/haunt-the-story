# H A U N T # T H E S T O R Y ## CHAPTER: 1 ### PART: 2 That flying creature returned and dragged the priest away into the darkness. After that, all of us were completely terrified. A few moments later, we heard strange noises coming from the assembly area. We slowly went there… and then we saw her. The girl was floating in the air… far above the ground, as if gravity had no effect on her. Her hair moved wildly in the cold wind, and her eyes glowed in the darkness. And then we saw something even worse. The priest suddenly started rising into the air too. His body jerked violently, as if some invisible force had grabbed him. He struggled to breathe, but only choking sounds escaped from his mouth. His eyes looked like they were about to burst out, and his hands moved helplessly in the air. The girl slowly floated closer to him. She leaned near his face and whispered only one sentence: **“You will not survive now.”** And in the very next second— **KRRRKKK—** The priest’s neck twisted violently… so far that it completely turned in the opposite direction. For one second, everything became completely silent. Then his lifeless body continued hanging in the air… eyes wide open… mouth half-open… as if he wanted to say something before dying. A moment later, his body dropped heavily onto the ground. The girl slowly turned toward us and smiled. **“Who’s next?”** At that moment, I realized this was not just a story anymore… it was a warning. We ran from there immediately because we knew we could do nothing against her. In front of that thing, we were nothing more than tiny insects she could crush whenever she wanted. When we reached the gate, the same old man was standing there again. But this time, he looked even stranger. He was wearing black glasses, and in a low voice he said, “She killed him, didn’t she? I knew it…” I finally lost my patience and shouted, “Who are you?! Where did you come from?! And how do you know all this?!” The old man only muttered, “I know… I know…” And after saying that, he simply walked away. We all returned to our homes in silence. Two days later, around 10 in the morning, I got a call from Ayush. He told me that Sai was missing. Nobody knew where he had gone. Hearing this, I rushed to Sai’s house. Everyone else was already there. We tried to calm Sai’s mother and promised her that we would find him. She had already filed a police complaint, and the police had started searching too. For two hours, we searched everywhere for Sai. We checked the abandoned school, the square, and even asked his girlfriend, but nobody had seen him. By evening, exhausted and hopeless, we were walking through Grand Park when we met DD there. We told her everything that had happened to us. Hearing all of it made her pale with fear. Then she told us something terrifying. A few days earlier, she had passed by the deserted road near the school during the evening. Darkness had slowly started swallowing the building. Suddenly, she looked toward the third-floor windows and saw a girl standing near one classroom window completely still. Half of her face was hidden in darkness, while the other half was lit by pale light. Her white, lifeless eyes stared directly at her. Then slowly… without moving her body at all… the girl turned her head completely around. Black liquid flowed from her eyes, and her lips moved as if she were whispering something in an ancient language. The air suddenly became freezing cold, and the sound of benches dragging echoed from inside the school. DD said she became completely terrified. The girl stared at her in such a horrifying way that it felt like she would kill her within seconds. Then suddenly the girl vanished from the window. But in the very next moment, DD heard breathing right beside her ear. A whisper said: **“You have seen me… now you cannot leave.”** When DD turned around, the entire school suddenly came alive. Every light inside the building switched on at once and started flickering violently. Blood-like handprints slowly appeared on the classroom windows from inside… as if someone was trying to escape. DD ran away from there, and even now she was still terrified. After hearing her story, it felt like the ground disappeared beneath our feet. None of us could understand how all this was possible. After some time, DD said she should leave because it was getting late, and then she went home. We were completely shaken after hearing everything. None of us could think properly anymore. After a while, we all returned to our homes. The next day, I got a phone call from DD’s house. Her sister answered. In a trembling voice, she said, “Someone took DD…” My entire body froze. “W-What?! How did this happen? When?!” I asked nervously. Her sister explained what happened during the night. Everyone in the house was sleeping when suddenly there was a massive explosion-like sound, as if a wall inside the house had been torn apart. The ground shook violently, and at the same moment, a horrifying scream came from DD’s room — so loud and painful that it felt like their ears would bleed. They ran toward her room. The moment they opened the door, a freezing wave of rotten air hit their faces. Inside, the entire wall near the window had been destroyed. Bricks and debris were floating unnaturally in the air, as if time itself had frozen. And then they saw it. A black smoke-like creature floating in the air. It had no proper face… only twisted claws and glowing red eyes. DD herself was floating above the ground beside it. Her hair hung upside down, her eyes had turned white, and her mouth was stretched open wider than any human’s should be. The creature was choking her without even touching her, as if some invisible force had wrapped around her neck. DD’s screams slowly turned into laughter… a horrifying laugh that felt like it could tear apart a person’s soul. Blood-like marks started appearing across the walls, and black insects began falling from the ceiling. Her family screamed and tried to help her, but their bodies wouldn’t move. It felt as though their feet had become stuck to the floor. And then, within seconds… both DD and the black creature disappeared into the darkness. Only one final voice remained echoing through the room: **“She will not survive now.”** And after that… they heard one last scream from DD. Then everything became silent. Hearing all this made my entire soul tremble with fear. That evening, I called all my friends to Grand Park. When we met there, I told them everything that had happened to DD. Hearing it made all of them shiver. Slowly, darkness spread across the park. The silence of the night became heavier and heavier. Ansh spoke in a trembling voice, “I told you before… none of this is normal. The girl from that story… she has returned.” Harsh nervously said, “How did both DD and Sai disappear in the same night? And every single time, that same girl is connected to it. This can’t just be a coincidence.” Then Ayush said, “And what about Sai? That girl possessed him before too. Remember? His eyes became empty, his voice changed… and then one night he vanished without a trace.” Ansh took a deep breath and said, “Yes… and after he was possessed, Sai kept repeating only one thing: ‘I will not spare those boys… and whoever sees me will die.’” Harsh lowered his voice even more. “What happened with DD… the wall exploding, that flying black creature, and the girl’s laughter between DD’s screams… that girl isn’t just killing people anymore. She’s taking them away.” Ayush whispered fearfully, “So first Sai… then DD… who’s next?” Ansh answered in a heavy voice, “Maybe… the ones who know about her. The ones who have seen her. The ones telling her story.” The moment he said that, the air turned freezing cold. And from somewhere behind us, a soft whisper echoed: **“All of you…”** At that moment, we realized the girl was standing somewhere behind us… listening to everything. The park had completely changed. The place that was usually filled with children’s laughter during the day now felt dead. Only the cold wind and the sound of dry leaves scraping across the ground could be heard. Some streetlights were off, while others flickered weakly. Twisted shadows stretched across the ground beneath them. Ansh felt like the park itself was watching us. Slowly, he turned around… and immediately footprints began appearing in the dirt behind us. Bare footprints. One after another. Slowly coming closer. I grabbed his hand tightly. “Don’t turn around,” I whispered. “If you do… she’ll know.” Suddenly, the swings creaked on their own. One swing slowly moved back and forth even though there was no wind. A scraping metallic sound echoed from the slide, as if invisible fingers were dragging across it. Ayush whispered, “It’s her… that girl.” The darkness between the trees slowly gathered together in one place, as if the night itself was forming a shape. And then a crooked shadow emerged from within it. Long hair covered its face, its head tilted unnaturally, and its feet floated just above the ground. Its laughter was quiet… but terrifyingly close. **“All… of you…”** Ansh felt his body becoming heavier. He tried to move, but it felt as if the ground had trapped him. The colder the air became, the closer the girl’s presence felt behind us. Then suddenly— A glowing black line appeared across the ground. Within seconds, a dark circle formed around all four of us. The moment the girl tried stepping closer, she was thrown backward by some invisible force. Her laughter became louder and angrier. Then a cold voice echoed through the park: **“Not now.”** That voice did not belong to the girl. A tall shadow stepped out from between the trees. **Kalanga.** He stood there calmly, as if everything happening in the park was completely under his control. Strange black symbols floated around his fingers — symbols so unnatural that simply looking at them made our heads feel heavy. He began speaking in a language none of us understood. The sound itself felt sharp enough to cut through the air. The girl’s shadow trembled with rage. Her laughter slowly turned into screaming, but she still couldn’t cross the black circle. “Stay behind me,” Kalanga said coldly, keeping his eyes fixed on the darkness. The girl looked toward the swings one final time, where fresh footprints were still appearing in the dirt. Then, with a twisted smile, she melted back into the darkness between the trees… as if she had never been there at all. The air suddenly became normal again. The swings stopped moving. The footprints slowly disappeared. All four of us collapsed to our knees, breathing heavily as if we had just escaped drowning. Ansh asked in a shaking voice, “You… you saved us?” Kalanga looked at us expressionlessly. “I didn’t save you,” he replied. “I only bought you some time.” Harsh quickly said, “You knew she would come here.” Kalanga’s eyes remained fixed on the darkest corner of the park. “That’s why I warned you to stay away from the school.” Ayush gathered courage and asked, “What about DD and Sai… are they alive?” Kalanga didn’t answer directly. “The thing that took them,” he said slowly, “could have taken all of you too.” The streetlights fully turned on again, and the park slowly started looking normal once more. All of us were shocked because the man who had saved us was the same old man who had tried to stop us from entering the school before. Ayush quietly whispered, “I told you… this old man knows everything.” Before I could say anything, the swing far behind us slowly started moving again… as if someone invisible had touched it. The air inside the park suddenly became completely still. Not a single leaf moved. Not even the branches swayed. It felt as if the entire place had frozen. A strange pressure filled the air, making it difficult to breathe. Ansh felt his ears ringing. “Can you hear that?” Aryan asked. “Only breathing,” Harsh replied nervously. “My own breathing.” Then suddenly, the ground cracked open violently. Bare footprints appeared in the dirt again — but this time they weren’t moving straight. They moved in circles, surrounding the entire park. Ayush suddenly screamed, “My legs!” His feet were sinking into the ground. The earth had become sticky, almost alive, as if invisible hands were dragging him downward. Ansh and Harsh pulled him back with all their strength. At that exact moment, a horrifying scream echoed from above. The scream was so powerful that the streetlights exploded one after another. The park drowned in darkness. Only one place remained lit — near the swings. And there… the girl finally appeared completely. This time she wasn’t floating. She stood upright, though her feet still didn’t touch the ground. Her eyes were completely black. No pupils. Only darkness. Her mouth slowly stretched open far wider than normal. Her jaw cracked loudly as it bent downward unnaturally. “I will never spare you,” she said. But her voice no longer sounded human. It sounded like many voices speaking together at once. The trees themselves bent toward her. The chains of the swings twisted like snakes. Then she raised her hand. Every bench, dustbin, and slide in the park suddenly lifted into the air. “RUN!” Aryan screamed. But there was nowhere left to escape. One bench flew directly toward Harsh — but suddenly stopped midair. A massive impact shook the ground. Burning black symbols appeared across the earth. One after another. Circles within circles. It felt as though the entire park had been sealed. “ENOUGH!” Kalanga’s voice thundered through the darkness. He stood between us and the girl, but this time even his eyes glowed black. Between his hands swirled a rotating mass of dark energy that pulled the air itself inward. The girl screamed in fury. Trees began snapping apart. The ground cracked again. She slashed her claws through the air, and invisible attacks rushed toward Kalanga from every direction. But Kalanga did not move back even a single step. He slammed both hands onto the ground. The entire park shook violently. “You do not belong here,” Kalanga said coldly. The girl laughed loudly. “I am already dead,” she replied. “And the dead do not follow rules anymore.” Suddenly her body twisted unnaturally in half. One face looked forward while another faced backward. Her neck continued rotating endlessly. Ansh shut his eyes in terror. But the moment she tried moving forward, she crashed into an invisible barrier. This time, her scream sounded filled with pain. “You cannot kill me,” Kalanga said calmly. “And I cannot stop you from leaving… not yet.” Then he looked toward us. “But these boys…” The girl slowly stared at all of us. A horrifying smile spread across her face. “I only want them,” she whispered. “Do not interfere… or you will die too.” And in the next second… She sank straight into the ground. As if she had never existed at all. Slowly, the darkness faded away. The streetlights turned back on — some broken, some still flickering weakly. The park once again looked normal.
